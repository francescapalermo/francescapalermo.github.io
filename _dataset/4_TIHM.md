**TIHM** (Technology Integrated Health Management) is a comprehensive dataset developed to support research in **remote healthcare monitoring**, particularly for people living with dementia. The dataset was described in *Scientific Data* (2023) and includes multi-modal data collected continuously from in-home sensors and clinical observations, covering activity, sleep, physiological measurements, and labelled health events. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

TIHM was collected as part of a feasibility study and is openly available for researchers interested in machine learning, digital biomarkers, longitudinal health monitoring, and early detection of clinically relevant events. 

### Information

The dataset comprises:

- Passive in-home sensor data (motion, door sensors) capturing daily activity  
- Sleep monitoring data collected via under-the-mattress sensors  
- Physiological measurements (heart rate, blood pressure, body temperature, etc.)  
- Time-aligned labels for health-related events and alerts validated by clinical teams  
- Demographic metadata for participants in the study  

TIHM enables analyses of activity patterns, physiological trends, and behavioural changes over time, and supports predictive modelling for health events in dementia care. 

Official dataset and descriptor available at: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-023-02519-y
